Meet & Greet with Ali Adrian Rusmiputro & David Garcia

by Admin

Rider Ali Adrian Rusmiputro will represent Indonesia at the Moto2 European Championship event, part of the 2015 FIM CEV Spanish Road Racing Championship, riding a 650cc bike.

The 21-year-old Adrian has already made a name for himself on the Indonesian motorbike racing circuit, having competed in many national competitions since he was 14 years old, after first developing an interest in racing bikes when he was 3 years old.

Sponsored by state-owned oil and gas company PT Pertamina, Adrian is under tight supervision by his mentor, former Moto Grand Prix (MotoGP) racer and owner of International Circuito de Almeria, David Garcia. The event was hosted by Sandiaga Uno, owner of Saratoga Group and Federal Oil. Sandy possessed strong confidence in Adrian's effort to be Indonesia's future hope of the motor racing series. "I am personally having a strong believe in this young man's determination (to reach his aspirations), and we will do anything we could to support Adrian and make Indonesia proud."

"All of the hard work and the achievements I have notched up before this opportunity were only the beginning of my dream to make history as the first Indonesian racer to ultimately compete in the MotoGP. I aim to place at least in the top five at the Moto2 race," Adrian said confidently at a Meet & Greet session at Workout Lounge, Senayan Driving Range on Tuesday (Jan. 27).

Sat proudly alongside Adrian, Garcia praised the young racer for showing immense determination and passion for the sport, confident that Adrian would make it to the MotoGP races.

"I am not going to give Adrian unnecessary pressures, all I want is that he could follow my plans. Adrian should do each procedures step by step. Most importantly, how to avoid him being involved in too many crashes, because it can disturb the plans.
"His first race will be on April 26 in Portimao, Portugal, and will be followed by races in Le Mans, France, before he enters an all-Spanish race in Barcelona, Alcaniz, Albacete, Navarra and Jerez. The last race is set to be in Valencia on Nov. 15. I am confident that Adrian will make history in the upcoming events," he concludes.

On the same occasion, Adrian's father, Erin Rusmiputro, also expresses his great hope towards his son. He hope that Adrian will always maintain his humbleness, because it is the basic ingredient for success. "Beside that, he should always follow what his coach asks him to do, in terms of daily exercises and also mentality. That way, Adrian will be ready to compete in the European atmosphere."

Adrian is currently on a one-year contract with Pertamina and a seven-year contract under Garcia. As part of his preparation, Adrian will also perform several winter tests starting on February 14, 2015.
